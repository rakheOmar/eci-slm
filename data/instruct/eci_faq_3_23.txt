User: Why EVM Power pack status is shown as 99% in some CUs even after full day use in poll?
Assistant: The EVM power pack is designed to support 4 BUs along with 1 CU with 2000 votes being cast. The voltage falls very gradually when the capacity is high but falls rapidly when the battery capacity reduces below a threshold. In case of lighter current load like with a single BU and less than 1000 votes being cast, the battery current drain is low and output voltage may not drop below 7.4V and can thus so display of 99% capacity.

User: What are the characteristics of EVM power packs, their installation and functioning of EVM at different output voltages. (Part 1)
Assistant: Alkaline cells are used in Power Packs of EVM. These cells are non-rechargeable, and have long shelf-life and high energy density.

EVM Power Packs are for single use as EVM requires power supply only for its functioning during elections and not during the long storage periods in between elections.

EVM is designed to work satisfactorily with battery pack voltage ranging from 5.5V to 8.2V, and within this range EVM functioning is independent of operating voltage.
The output voltage of a new power pack for CU ranges from 7.5 V to 8.0V. Hence the battery status of an CU power pack with an output voltage greater than 7.4 V is initialised to 99%, when it is switched ON.

When power pack voltage becomes less than 5.8 V, the CU displaying flashes ‘change battery’. Below 5.5V, EVM does not function; however, data stored in EVM remains intact.

The current consumption of the EVM depends on number of BUs used and number of votes cast.

Alkaline cells used in EVM power pack have the characteristics of regaining some voltage in power-OFF condition. If battery voltage increases to >7.4V during storage, then when CU is switched on during counting, it initialises the available battery capacity to 99%.

Power Packs in EVMs are installed in EVMs and sealed in the presence of candidates/their representatives during commissioning of EVMs. In case of replacement of power pack on poll day, if required, the same is replaced in presence of polling agents of the candidates and sealed again.

User: Whether EVM functioning is independent of operating voltage?
Assistant: EVM is designed to work satisfactorily with power pack voltage ranging from 5.5V to 8.2V, and within this range EVM functioning is independent of the available voltage. On every ‘power on’ Control Unit measures the Power Pack voltage. When power pack voltage becomes less than 5.8 V, the CU displays flashes ‘change battery’. Below 5.5V, EVM does not function; however, data stored in EVM remains intact. The nominal (initial) voltage of a new Power Pack is 7.5V-8.0V (1.5-1.6VX5 as five cells are used in series to make the EVM power pack and output voltage of each cell is 1.5-1.6 V). Subsequently, the battery capacity used up for every operation is reduced from the available battery capacity. When battery voltage becomes less than 5.8 V or available capacity reduces below 10%, the CU flashes ‘change battery’. In such case, CU power pack is replaced in the presence of polling agents of the candidates and a record is maintained in a prescribed form. CU power pack is sufficient to meet the requirements of power consumption of the EVM during poll day and counting day. The sealed battery compartment and constant monitoring of EVM during storage, as explained in point 5 above, prevent any surreptitious change of battery and ensure safety of the EVM. Below 5.5V, EVM does not function; however, data stored in EVM remains safe.

Polled EVMs are stored in two-layered security cordons, apart from other non-force and transparency measures. EVM storage room is opened for any purpose like movement, FLC, commissioning, dispersal for poll, storage of polled and reserve EVMs, counting of votes etc., only in the presence of the representatives of the recognized political parties/candidates under videography.
In the past elections, some instances had been noticed through local newspapers etc. that EVMs were found in hotel or private premises, etc. All such cases were examined thoroughly and found to be related to reserve EVMs and attributable to human mistakes. The factual instances were brought to the notice of the candidates/media to clear their doubt. If any violation of ECI instructions is found, disciplinary action is taken against the Sector Officer/ Polling Officer concerned.
It is to be noted that reserve EVMs (with designated pink/ white stickers on carrycases) are provided to Sector Officers, on poll day, with security and their vehicles move as per given route chart. Movement of vehicles is also monitored through GPS/Mobile based App tracking. The list of reserve EVMs are also provided to all the candidates for reference and transparency.

Assistant: In the presence of candidates/counting agents appointed by them, the total votes recorded in EVM as mentioned in the Form-17 C is tallied with the total votes displayed on the display panel of the Control Unit.

If the winning margin is equal or less than the votes recorded in such machines, VVPAT slips of the respective machine is counted. However, such cases are rare and are only due to human error by the polling personnel and not due to any technical issue of EVMs.

This is the standard codified procedure. Further reference may be made to the Manual on Electronic Voting Machine.

(i) The margin of error in the report of the Committee was put as 2%. Regarding the first point-- A general perception is that a small sample will not be able to make any valid inference about a large population. This of course is completely erroneous as any of the suggested figures of 10% or 30% or 50% has no statistical basis whatsoever and the numbers have no sanctity. The accuracy of the results obtained by a sampling procedure mainly depends on the ‘absolute sample size’ and not on ‘the sample size as a percentage of the population size.’ While non-intuitive this is the standard statistical result and is well validated in all standard books on Sampling Theory. While it is true that a 2% margin of error has been considered in the report but the confidence level considered is also far higher at 99.993665752%(4 sigma level) than considered by most commentators. Similarly, the question about what to use as the population for the study? To answer this question, it was first necessary to decide over the level at which the statistical guarantee was required.
